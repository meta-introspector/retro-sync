1. Artists
Upload music to your platform
Music is distributed automatically to Spotify, Apple Music, and other streaming services via DDEX integration
Earn revenue from streams on external platforms
Pay a 2.7% fee only when cashing out (micro-fee ensures fairness)

Flow:

Music uploaded → Distributed to Spotify/Apple Music/etc. → Streams generate revenue → Artist cashes out → 2.7% fee applied → 90% nodes / 10% platform
2. Platform
Hosts the distribution service and manages integrations
Earns revenue in two ways:
10% cut of the 2.7% fee when artists cash out
Seeding files itself (providing storage and availability)
Uses earnings to maintain the platform, servers, software, and operations

Flow:

Artist/user fee revenue + seeding rewards → Platform operational budget → Profit for platform owner
3. P2P Nodes
Run the network, host, and seed music files
Earn 90% of the 2.7% micro-fee from withdrawals and streams
Can earn extra from seeding popular or high-demand files
Incentivized for:
High uptime
Fast response times
Serving more requests

Flow:

Artist/user pays fee → 90% distributed to nodes → Node rewards for hosting & uptime → Nodes reinvest or cash out
4. Users / Listeners
Listen to music via the platform
Pay a small 2.7% micro-fee per stream
Fee helps keep P2P nodes incentivized and the network fast and reliable

Flow:

Listener plays track → Micro-fee collected → 90% to nodes / 10% to platform → Network reliability maintained → Artists’ content delivered smoothly
5. Overall Ecosystem Flow

✅ Key Takeaways
Artist-first → minimal upfront costs, earnings from existing streams
Sustainable network → micro-fees fund nodes for reliability
Platform earns fairly → small cut + seeding, enough to operate
Aligned incentives → everyone in the ecosystem benefits:
Artists: cash-out revenue
Nodes: hosting rewards
Users: fast, reliable music delivery
Platform: sustainable operations
